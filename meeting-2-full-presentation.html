What We Heard — March 17th

Here’s what you told us.
Let’s make sure we got it right.

These are the notes we captured from your discovery call. Everything we show you today is built around this.

Q1 — Organization Structure
How is your marketing org structured today?
Lean org with 4 brand teams (whiskey, clear spirits, premium/artisanal, foundation/legacy); roles from coordinator to brand director with category VPs; separate creative services team (~10 people) + agency Stella for overflow. Lynn’s procurement team mirrors brand structure and operates as embedded resources within brand teams. Gap identified in true trade marketing — 2 new commercial program managers added to bridge national-to-local.
Q2 — Program Development
How are trade programs developed and rolled out?
Commercial program managers localize national programs through a sales lens. Sales teams frequently request items outside national parameters — pulling procurement off priority work. Locally produced materials often don’t meet national creative standards, creating inconsistency across markets.
Q3 — Tools & Systems
What systems support planning, tracking, and measuring today?
Current SRS (Sales Resource Site) handles inventory and ordering. No centralized tracking exists — locally-funded SAM activation spend is largely untracked and never recapped centrally. Distributor co-op spend creates a third unmonitored layer with no single view across brand A&P, local SAM, and co-op.
Q4 — POS Pricing Confidence
How confident are you in fair market value on POS spend?
Somewhat confident — multi-bid process in place nationally and at scale; trusted supplier relationships on standard programs. Confidence drops on one-offs, fast-turnarounds, and new-to-world requests where vendor options are limited and pricing is taken on faith.
Q5 — What You’re Proudest Of
What gets the best feedback from your team?
Communication & collaboration — brand teams, sales teams, and supplier evaluations consistently praised. Procurement is seen as a true extension of the brand team. Creativity is constantly recognized; the supplier evaluation process actively brings new creative value to brand teams.
Q6 — The Big One ★
What’s the one thing you’d most want to see improve?
National vs. local tension — sales bypasses procurement, funds locally through SAM budgets with no tracking, creating unmonitored spend across brand A&P, local, and distributor co-op. Legacy vendor and shipping relationships are a Louisville-rooted differentiator they don’t want to lose — the challenge is leveraging them as the org matures without the current SRS platform holding them back.

The honest answer
Can brandhub track SAM and co-op spend automatically?
brandhub provides the capability — Order Windows, programs, approval workflows, reporting. But if a sales rep charges something to a SAM budget outside the platform, it won't appear in the dashboard. The platform creates the on-ramp. Your org decides who's required to use it. That's a ways-of-working decision, not a software setting. We can help you think through the rollout, but we'd never promise a tool solves a governance problem on its own.
